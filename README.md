## 📊 Project Overview
This project presents a comprehensive analysis of **Scope 1 and Scope 2 carbon emissions** of Union Bank PLC using real operational data.

The objective of this project is to:
- Measure carbon emissions
- Identify high emission sources (hotspots)
- Analyze electricity consumption patterns
- Forecast future emissions
- Recommend sustainability strategies

This project combines **Data Analysis + Forecasting + Dashboard Visualization** to support real-world decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Key Objectives
- Quantify Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions
- Analyze carbon footprint (2024 vs 2025)
- Identify emission hotspots across branches
- Perform electricity consumption analysis
- Forecast future emissions (2026)
- Develop emission reduction strategies

---

## 🧠 Key Insights
- Electricity is the **largest contributor** to emissions  
- Total emissions increased from **1503.72 → 1540.46 tCO₂e**  
- ~20% of locations contribute to **~80% of electricity usage** (Pareto principle)  
- Forecast shows emissions will **continue increasing** if no action is taken  
- Combined strategy gives the **best emission reduction**

## 📊 Analysis Performed

### 1. Carbon Emission Analysis
- Scope 1 → Diesel, Vehicles, Refrigerant  
- Scope 2 → Electricity  
- Emissions increased by **2.44% from 2024 to 2025**

---

### 2. Electricity Consumption Analysis
- Electricity is the **main emission driver**
- Offices & branches consume more than ATMs
- Increasing trend observed over time

---

### 3. Hotspot Identification
- Small number of branches contribute majority of emissions
- Head Office and major branches are key contributors

---

### 4. Forecasting (2026)
- Forecasted emissions ≈ **1577 tCO₂e**
- Shows steady upward trend

---

### 5. Scenario Analysis
| Scenario | Description |
|--------|-------------|

| Scenario 2 | Reduce refrigerant emissions |

## ✅ Recommendations

1. **Improve Electricity Management**  
   Electricity is the largest contributor, so reducing usage in high-consumption branches should be the top priority.

2. **Focus on High-Emission Locations**  
   Since a small number of branches contribute most emissions, targeted actions on these locations will give maximum impact.

3. **Strengthen Refrigerant Management**  
   Regular maintenance of air conditioning systems can reduce refrigerant leakage.

4. **Optimize Fuel Usage**  
   Improve efficiency in generators and company vehicles to reduce emissions.

5. **Implement Continuous Monitoring**  
   Use the Power BI dashboard to monitor energy usage and emissions regularly for better decision-making.

## 🧾 Conclusion

This project successfully analyzed the **carbon footprint of Union Bank PLC** using real operational data and statistical methods.

The results clearly show that:
- Carbon emissions are **increasing over time**
- Electricity consumption is the **main contributor**
- A small number of locations are responsible for the majority of emissions

The forecasting analysis indicates that emissions will continue to rise if no actions are taken. However, scenario analysis demonstrates that a **combined reduction strategy is the most effective approach** for minimizing future emissions.

Overall, this project highlights the importance of **data-driven decision-making** in sustainability and provides a strong foundation for improving energy efficiency and reducing carbon emissions.
